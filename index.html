Mumbai, India

Mumbai (formerly known as Bombay), India, is famous for its chaotic streets. For bargains and people-watching, outdoor bazaars top the list of attractions. Popular waterfront destinations are Marine Drive, where visitors go to watch the sun set over the Arabian Sea, and the carnival-like Juhu Beach. More sightseeing options are the Gandhi Museum, in the leader's former home, and the cave temples of Elephanta Island. For tranquillity, Mumbai has many religious sites, lakes and parks.



Places to Visit

  • Gateway Of India

    The Gateway of India is a historic arch-monument in Mumbai.

  • CST

    Chhatrapati Shivaji Terminus (CST) is a historic railway station in Mumbai.

  • Marine-Drive

    Marine Drive is a scenic coastal in Mumbai, offering stunning views of the Arabian Sea.

  • Colaba

    Colaba is a vibrant neighborhood in South Mumbai.

  • Elephanta caves

    The Elephanta Caves are ancient rock-cut temples on Elephanta Island near Mumbai.

  • Global Vipassana Pagoda

    The Global Vipassana Pagoda, is a massive meditation hall..

  • kidZania

    KidZania is an interactive, educational theme park for childrens.

  • Bandra-Worli-SeaLink

    The Bandra-Worli Sea Link is a cable-stayed bridge in Mumbai.

  • Shree Siddhivinayak Temple

    Siddhivinayak Temple in Mumbai is a revered Hindu temple dedicated to Lord Ganesha.

  • Snow-World

    Snow World Mumbai is an indoor snow park offering a winter-like experience with snow rides.
